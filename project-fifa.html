FIFA 23 Talent Distribution Analysis
Descriptive Analytics · Football Operations Domain · 2025
Overview

An end-to-end descriptive analysis of how FIFA 23 player ratings distribute across leagues, clubs, and positions — framed as a hypothetical football operations engagement. The project is built around a SQL-first pipeline with DuckDB, pre-committed cross-validation strategies, and explicit governance boundaries designed to prevent overclaiming on game-derived proxy data.

The stakeholder framing — scouting departments, team analysts, football operations leadership — is a narrative device that demonstrates how to structure, bound, and communicate descriptive findings in a domain where overclaiming is easy and dangerous.

Key Findings
  • Two distinct league archetypes identified: depth-based leagues (high median talent, low within-club spread) and elite-concentration leagues (moderate median, high spread driven by top-club outliers). Bundesliga and La Liga anchor the two patterns respectively.
  • Female player ratings systematically exceed male ratings at all 15 positions by 7–14 points — confirmed as a FIFA rating scale calibration difference, not a real-world quality comparison. The gap is structurally consistent and does not reverse at any position.
  • A 97.31% female league-attribution gap was formally documented as a structural data constraint, not a cleaning failure — restricting female analysis to position-level questions only.
  • Cross-validation strategies were pre-committed before examining outputs to prevent self-confirming analysis. All elevated findings required confirmation across both primary and independent validation queries.
What was refused

Four recommendations were formally refused because the dataset could not support them: causal claims about concentration drivers, financial efficiency recommendations (no wage or transfer data), league recommendations for the female population (97.31% attribution gap), and any claim treating FIFA ratings as predictive of real-world performance. These refusals are documented with the same rigour as the approved recommendations.
